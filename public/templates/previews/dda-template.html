Emicizumab-kxwh is a bispecific antibody that bridges Factor IXa and Factor X, substituting for the cofactor activity of activated Factor VIII — without requiring FVIII itself.
What Makes It Different
1
Bispecific Antibody
Simultaneously binds Factor IXa and Factor X, bringing them into proximity to activate Factor X.
2

Works regardless of inhibitor status — effective in patients with and without anti-FVIII inhibitors.
3
Subcutaneous Delivery
Convenient SC injection eliminates need for IV infusion, reducing treatment burden significantly.

Administration Steps
1
Preparation
Remove from refrigerator 15–30 min before use. Do not shake. Inspect for particulates.
2
Site Selection
Abdomen, upper arm, or thigh. Rotate injection sites. Avoid areas with skin lesions.
3
Injection
Insert needle at 45–90° angle. Inject slowly. Press firmly — do not rub injection site.
4

Refrigerate at 2–8°C (36–46°F). Protect from light. Do not freeze or shake.

Important Safety Information

Full ISI — Please Read
⚠ Warnings & Precautions

Thrombotic Microangiopathy (TMA): Cases of TMA, some serious, have been reported following use of emicizumab with activated prothrombin complex concentrate (aPCC; FEIBA). Monitor for signs/symptoms. Discontinue aPCC and hold emicizumab if TMA is diagnosed.

Thromboembolism: Serious thromboembolic events have been reported when emicizumab was used concomitantly with aPCC. Use aPCC with caution; avoid doses >100 U/kg/24 hours for >24 hours.

Contraindications

No contraindications are identified in the current U.S. Prescribing Information.

Adverse Reactions

The most common adverse reactions (≥5%) in clinical trials were:

  • Injection site reactions (pain, erythema, pruritus, swelling)
  • Headache
  • Arthralgia
  • Fatigue
  • Upper respiratory tract infection
Drug Interactions

Emicizumab may interfere with activated partial thromboplastin time (aPTT)-based clotting assays and Bethesda assays for FVIII inhibitors. Consult laboratory guidance for appropriate test selection in patients on emicizumab therapy.

Use in Specific Populations
  • Pregnancy: No adequate data. Based on mechanism of action, potential risk to fetus. Advise patients of reproductive potential.
  • Lactation: Unknown if present in human milk. Consider developmental and health benefits of breastfeeding.
  • Pediatric Use: Safety and efficacy established in patients ≥1 month of age (HAVEN 2).
  • Renal/Hepatic Impairment: No dose adjustment required for mild-to-moderate impairment.
Indication

HEMLIBRA® (emicizumab-kxwh) is indicated for routine prophylaxis to prevent or reduce the frequency of bleeding episodes in adults and pediatric patients with Hemophilia A with or without Factor VIII inhibitors.
