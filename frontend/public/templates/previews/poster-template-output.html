1 Background
Hemophilia A Prophylaxis

Hemophilia A with or without inhibitors requires effective prophylaxis to prevent joint damage and bleeding episodes. Hemlibra, a bispecific antibody mimicking factor VIII, demonstrated sustained efficacy in the HAVEN clinical program.

"Monthly subcutaneous prophylaxis represents a paradigm shift in hemophilia A management."
2 Methods
Study Design

HAVEN 4 enrolled adults and adolescents with hemophilia A (with or without FVIII inhibitors). Patients received emicizumab 3 mg/kg Q4W subcutaneously.

Primary Endpoint

The primary endpoint was annualized bleed rate (ABR).

5 Important Safety Information
WARNING: THROMBOTIC MICROANGIOPATHY (TMA) AND THROMBOEMBOLISM

Cases of TMA were reported when aPCC (>100 U/kg/24h cumulative) was administered to patients receiving Hemlibra.

6 Conclusions
  • Monthly subcutaneous Hemlibra prophylaxis provides sustained, near-elimination of bleeds in patients with hemophilia A.
  • Treatment burden is dramatically reduced from up to 156 annual IV infusions to just 13 subcutaneous injections per year.
  • Efficacy is consistent regardless of inhibitor status, with over half of patients achieving zero treated bleeds.
  • These results support emicizumab-kxwh as an effective prophylactic option for hemophilia A patients.
These data reinforce emicizumab-kxwh as a paradigm-shifting prophylactic option for hemophilia A patients.
